    DESCRIPTION

    ::PROJECT OBJECTIVES::

    The internet, and web pages themselves, were first created for the sole purpose of structuring and publishing static content in a boring and completely lifeless way (not really their goal, but they achieved it). The only form of interactivity available to users was to display inter-connected content and allow users to move between pages by clicking on hyperlinks and scrolling up and down. Clearly modern web technologies have moved past this.

    Our first project is intended to help us explore more immersive forms of interactivity on the web. We’ll be relying on tools we’ve learned about in the past and building upon them to respond to different forms of user input. To demonstrate this, we will be building sites that tell or re-tell a story. This story could be a fairy tale, a poem, a movie, or something you make up. The site should include at least 5 types of meaningful user input or engagement (click, drag, scroll, animation, etc.) and have at least 5 pages or sections of content for users to explore. At the same time, the site should maintain a cohesive visual language that encompasses and emphasizes the set as a whole.
